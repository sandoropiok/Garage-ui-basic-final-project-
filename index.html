1969 Mercedes-Benz 280SL

The 1969 Mercedes-Benz 280SL, part of the W 113 series and famously known as the "Pagoda," is a classic roadster celebrated for its timeless design and superior engineering. Powered by a 2.8-liter inline-six engine, it offers a perfect blend of performance and luxury. With its unique concave hardtop, the 280SL remains an iconic symbol of Mercedes-Benz craftsmanship and elegance.

1971 Lamborghini Miura P400 SV

The 1971 Lamborghini Miura P400 SV is the pinnacle of the Miura series and a masterpiece of automotive design. Renowned as one of the first true supercars, the P400 SV features a powerful 4.0-liter V12 engine, delivering exceptional performance and speed. With its sleek, low-slung body and innovative engineering, the Miura SV represents the height of Lamborghini's craftsmanship, blending stunning aesthetics with groundbreaking performance.

1970 Alfa Romeo GT 1300
